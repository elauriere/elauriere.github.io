I am currently a student in the European Doctoral School of Demography (EDSD) program, spending two months at the MPIDR in Rostock and nine months at SGH in Warsaw. I hold a Bachelor's degree in Mathematics and Computer Science Applied to Social Sciences, and a Master's degree in Data Analysis for Development Economics.

Through my academic journey, I developed strong skills in quantitative methods, particularly causal inference and multivariate statistical analysis. During my Master's, I applied these methods in the context of low- and middle-income countries (LMICs), an experience that confirmed my interest in issues related to developing countries.. It was also during this time that I discovered my interest in demography, through an internship at the French Institute for Demographic Studies (INED).

My main research interests lie at the intersection of demography and economics, focusing on child labour and children's domestic work, with particular attention to the place of girls in both. My work also engages critically with the definition of child labour itself, and includes the development of a tool (CLD-INED) aimed at pushing the boundaries of what can be analyzed in this field.
