Close to 138 million children aged 5 to 17 around the world are in child labour, according to a joint report published by the ILO and UNICEF in 2025. Sub-Saharan Africa remains the most strongly affected region, with 87 million children there in child labour. However, it is also an issue in certain regions of Europe, such as the Balkans. These estimates, moreover, are based on a strictly economic definition of work and exclude a large proportion of the domestic work performed by children. The Child Labour Database (CLD– INED), on the other hand, highlights the significant engagement of children across the globe in household chores, as well as the large workload that such chores represent for children, particularly girls.
